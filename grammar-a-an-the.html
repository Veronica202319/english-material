Using A, An, and The

A and An are indefinite articles used to refer to something unspecific, while The is a definite article used to refer to something specific.

  • A: Use before words that start with a consonant sound. Example: "I saw a dog."
  • An: Use before words that start with a vowel sound. Example: "I ate an apple."
  • The: Use when referring to a specific item. Example: "The sun is bright."
