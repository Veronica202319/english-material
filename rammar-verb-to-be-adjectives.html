Verb to Be + Adjectives

The verb to be (am, is, are) is often used with adjectives to describe people, things, or places.

  • Positive: "She is happy."
  • Negative: "He is not tall."
  • Question: "Are you tired?"

Examples:

  • I am hungry.
  • They are excited about the trip.
  • Is he friendly?
